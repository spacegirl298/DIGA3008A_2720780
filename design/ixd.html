IXD Process

Goal Alignment

My goal is to create a portfolio website that strikes a thoughtful balance between readability, creativity, and aesthetic appeal to deliver an engaging and professional user experience. I aim to prioritize legible typography, well-structured content, and intuitive navigation to ensure accessibility and clarity. The design will reflect my personal aesthetic through cohesive color schemes, balanced layouts, and carefully selected interactive elements—enhancing visual interest without overwhelming the user.

This website will serve as a professional representation of who I am as both a creative and a designer. It is tailored to attract potential employers and clients within the art, game design, and web development industries. By showcasing my drawings, paintings, games, logo designs, and web development process, the site will function as a comprehensive portfolio that highlights my skills, creativity, and professional identity.

Content Mapping

The website is structured into six main content areas, each serving a specific purpose and audience. The About Me section offers a personal introduction, providing insight into who I am and sharing some of my background and experiences. The Design section is divided into three key categories: the IxD Process, which includes goal alignment, content mapping, user flow, and explanations of interface elements; the Style Process, which explores color schemes, typography, composition, and includes a reference list; and Wireframes, which presents the structural layout of the site with annotated wireframes that highlight interaction points and design rationale. The Essays section contains academic writing that supports my creative and theoretical approach, while the Blogs section features research entries and reflections on my design process. Finally, the Portfolios section showcases my creative work and is organized into three categories—Games, Animation, and Art—providing a comprehensive overview of my skills and artistic output.

User Flow

The user flow of the website is thoughtfully structured, emphasizing intuitive navigation, consistent access, and clear pathways between content areas. The Home Page serves as the central navigation hub, allowing users to easily access all primary sections of the site: About Me, Design Process, Blogs, Essays, and Portfolio. This centralized structure helps users orient themselves quickly and move seamlessly from one section to another. Additionally, the pages are cross-linked, enabling fluid transitions and encouraging deeper exploration and engagement throughout the site.

Explaining Interface Elements

To ensure that I have a well-structured, visually appealing and user-friendly interface that aligns with my goals, I need to focus on the interface’s elements.

Navigation Bar

There is a consistent navigation bar present across all pages of the website, providing direct links to the primary sections. This consistency facilitates easy navigation and helps users clearly understand the site’s structure, enhancing overall usability.

Back to Top Button

The button enhances user experience by allowing quick return to the top of the page, whcih especially beneficial for pages with extensive content, such as the essays, blogs and design pages.

Footer

This visually communicates to the user that this is the end of page, but also has easily accessible information about me, such as my LinkedIn profile and Itch page.

Buttons

On the Design and Blog pages, navigation buttons are included to help users move between different sections with ease. These buttons enhance functionality by providing quick, intuitive access to related content, supporting a smoother and more efficient browsing experience.

Breadcrumb Navigation

The blog page contains breadcrumb navigation which is a secondary navigation system that provides links to previous pages without having to click the back button. If you are on a certain page, the breadcrumb will visually show that you are on that page, by not having a link to that page anymore.

About Me

The website contains an ID tag to the about me page. The about me page is located in the middle of the home page. I have added an ID tag, which links the user straight to where about me is. This allows for users to not get frustrated by being sent to the home page and then have to search for the about me. This link is in all navigations of the website. With Javascript, there is a smooth scroll to the about me section, which adds a satisfying appeal to the website.

Reflection

I have learnt that the IxD process is a very important part of the design process. At first I didn't understand the importance of the IxD process, until I started coding the CSS for my website. It allows for you to plan out your website and to ensure that you are creating a website that is user friendly and visually appealing. I have also learnt that it is important to focus on the content of the website, as this is what will attract users to your website. This process has been a great learning experience for me, helping me broaden my skills.
