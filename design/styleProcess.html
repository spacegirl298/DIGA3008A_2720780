Style Process

Defining Visual Aesthetic

The website features a clean and minimalist aesthetic with a distinctly vintage undertone, conveyed through a rich, moody color palette of blacks, dark reds, greys, and whites. This combination creates a dramatic yet refined backdrop that enhances the sculptural imagery showcased throughout the site. The layout is straightforward and gallery-like, allowing the art and writing pieces to stand out without visual distraction. Typography and interface elements are kept simple and unobtrusive, reinforcing the minimalist approach while complementing the site's classic, slightly nostalgic atmosphere.

Fonts

The combination of Cinzel, Cormorant Garamond, and Roboto creates a harmonious typographic blend that balances classic elegance with modern clarity. Cinzel brings a bold, sculptural presence to headings, Cormorant Garamond adds refined, vintage warmth to supporting text, and Roboto ensures clean, easy readability for navigation and body content. Together, they reinforce the website’s artistic and minimalist vintage aesthetic while maintaining strong usability. Together, these fonts respect web typography best practices by creating a clear hierarchy: Cinzel commands attention, Cormorant Garamond enriches with personality, and Roboto ensures usability and accessibility. This layered approach enhances both the aesthetic appeal and functionality of the website, helping users navigate content easily while enjoying a visually cohesive experience.

Colour Analysis

The primary palette of the website contains black (#000000), deep burgundy (#470b10), and w hite (#ffffff), it creates a bold and sophisticated look. The high contrast between black and white ensures clear readability and clean design, while the rich burgundy adds warmth and depth, serving as a striking accent color. Together, these colors balance elegance with emotional intensity, making the site feel vintage, professional, and inviting without overwhelming the user.

The deep red (#750011) adds emphasis and draws user attention with its passionate tone. The soft gray (#b7b7b7) is applied to navigation bars and borders for links, providing a subtle, neutral contrast that keeps the interface clean and easy to navigate without competing with the primary colors. This balance helps create a clear visual hierarchy and smooth user experience.

Composition

The visual hierarchy of the website is established through a clear and intentional use of typography. Each page begins with a large, centered headline in the Cinzel font, immediately drawing the viewer’s attention. This bold typographic choice sets the tone and indicates the primary message or theme of the page.

Beneath the main heading, subheadings appear in a slightly smaller size, using the Cormorant Garamond font, and are distinguished by underlining. This creates a strong secondary level of importance that is easy to recognize. Sub-subheadings use the Roboto font, styled in both italics and bold, to provide additional emphasis while maintaining a consistent visual language. The main body text also uses Roboto, offering readability and a modern, clean appearance that complements the more decorative fonts used for headings.

This progression helps users intuitively understand the structure of information. The eyes naturally gravitate toward the largest elements first, then move downward through the page, guided by changes in font size, style, and weight. This layered approach to typography enhances the user experience by visually signaling the relative importance of each piece of content.

Interface elements

Navigation Bar

Originally, my website featured a light grey navigation bar with black text, which created a strong contrast against the dark background and suited the modern, boxy aesthetic of the overall design. However, when I decided to shift the website’s visual direction to a more vintage and creative style, I updated the navigation bar to reflect this change. I replaced the clean, minimal lines with a more decorative, fancy line to mark the end of the navigation bar, adding a touch of character and aligning with the new aesthetic. Additionally, I changed the font to one that better fits the creative and nostalgic feel of the site, ensuring that the navigation bar not only remained functional but also became a cohesive part of the website’s new identity.

Footer Page

I updated the footer by replacing the text links with icons to create a cleaner and more visually appealing design. Using icons improves the user experience by making navigation quicker and more intuitive, as users can easily recognize familiar symbols. This also saves space, making the footer less cluttered while maintaining full functionality. Overall, the switch to icons enhances both the aesthetics and usability of the footer.

JavaScript Implementation

Portfolio Lightbox

In my portfolio section, I implemented a lightbox feature to enhance user interaction and provide a more informative experience. By clicking on an image, users can now view a larger version along with additional context and relevant links. This approach allows visitors to explore the work in more detail without navigating away from the main page. The lightbox not only improves visual presentation but also offers a more engaging and seamless way to access project information directly from the portfolio.

Back to Top Button

I added a "Back to Top" button to improve navigation and enhance the user experience on my website. This feature allows users to quickly return to the top of the page with a single click, eliminating the need for excessive scrolling, especially on longer pages. It's a simple yet effective usability enhancement that helps prevent user frustration and keeps the browsing experience smooth and efficient.

About Me Scroll

In my homepage, the About Me section is centrally positioned to ensure it's easily accessible and highlights key personal and professional details. To improve navigation and user experience, I added a smooth scroll transition that activates when users click the About Me link. This feature guides visitors directly to the section in an intuitive and visually appealing way, creating a more seamless and engaging browsing experience.

Fades Between Blogs

In the blogs section of my site, I incorporated a short fade transition to create a smoother and more visually appealing user experience. This subtle effect occurs when navigating between different blog entries, helping to reduce the abruptness of content changes. The fade transition enhances the overall flow of the site, making it feel more polished and engaging while maintaining a professional and user-friendly interface.

Hamburger Menu in Blogs

On the blogs page, I implemented a hamburger menu to improve navigation and user experience. This menu can be easily opened and closed, providing quick access to all blog entries without cluttering the main interface. It helps keep the page clean and organized, especially on smaller screens, while ensuring users can seamlessly browse through the available content. The functionality supports a responsive design, making the blog section more accessible and user-friendly.

Cursor Change

I attempted to implement a cursor change effect when users hover over certain portfolio pieces to improve the interactivity and give clear visual feedback. However, despite trying various methods, the cursor style didn’t change as expected. Although the functionality didn’t work, the effort reflects my intention to enhance user experience by making interactive elements more noticeable.

Reflection

Creating this style guide was a challenging yet deeply rewarding experience. Initially, I felt that my website didn’t truly reflect who I am as a person. I wasn’t proud of it, and I realized it wasn’t something I wanted people in the industry to see. That prompted me to completely rethink and redesign the site to create a look and feel that felt authentic to me, something I could proudly showcase.

I began by brainstorming the overall aesthetic, focusing on balancing creativity with readability to ensure the website would engage visitors while remaining professional. I explored various design elements, including color palettes, typography, and layout options. This process involved refining my ideas until I found a style that genuinely represented me and aligned with my goals for the site.

Through this journey, I learned the importance of being flexible and open to iteration. Seeing the website come alive in a real environment gave me better insight into spacing, alignment, and how different components interact visually. Ultimately, this process pushed me to adapt my style guide to meet practical needs without losing sight of my original vision. The result is a website that feels truly mine, one I’m proud to present to the world.
