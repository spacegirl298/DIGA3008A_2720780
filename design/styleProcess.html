Style Process

Defining Visual Aesthetic

My website will showcase a sleek, modern and immersive look using dark tones for the background. There will be pastel colours which contrast against the dark background. This contrast of colours, will enhance readability, ensuring that the text and UI remains clear. This use of the colour palate showcases creativity, as the dark background will allow for the colourful visuals to stand out. This will allow for my portfolio work to stand out.

Fonts

The fonts selected for my website are chosen with readability and user experience in mind. For main headings, I‚Äôve used Footlight MT Light, which features elegant, thin letterforms. This font brings a classic, professional feel while adding a creative edge through its subtle curves.

For sub-headings, I selected Palatino Linotype which strikes a balance between modernity and tradition. It is slightly simpler than the main heading font, creating a clear visual distinction between hierarchy levels.

For body text, I chose Aptos (Body), a highly legible sans-serif typeface. Its clean structure ensures effortless reading, which is essential since the body text carries the bulk of the content. The simplicity of Aptos helps prevent user fatigue, especially during longer reading sessions, and supports overall accessibility and ease of use.

Colour Analysis

The primary palette of the website consists of pastel greens and blues, chosen to reflect the brand's calm, creative idenity. These soft tones promote a clean and approachable aesthetic while avoiding harsh visual contrast. The dark blue background serves as a stable foundation, creating strong contrast with the lighter pastel elements, improving readability and making content stand out clearly.

Initially, a secondary pallate was included; however, it visually competed with the rest of the design. I chose to remove it to maintain consistency and avoid distracting the user. Too many colors can confuse users or draw attention away from important elements. By refining the palette, users are guided more intuitively through the site, improving both focus and the overall user experience.

Composition

The composition of my website will focus on clarity, engagement and creative flow. The website will contain whitespace to reduce clutter, give users room to process information and make elements stand out against the dark background. Avoiding clutter, helps guisde the users attention to what matters most. To create a visual hierarchy, headings will be large to stand out compared to text and images. Different headings have different fonts and sizes, visually showing to the user the different levels of the hierarchy. There will be adequate spacing in between text to ensure readability. This is especially needed on longer pages, particularly on mobile device to make the experiences feel more breathable.

Interface elements

Navigation Bar

The navigation bar will contain a light grey colour with black text, which will create a contrast from the dark background of the website. The navigation will reach across the top of the page. If the user is on a certain page in the website, its heading in the navigation bar will be highlighted in a different colour, visually showing the user which page they are on. The image above shows my original idea for the navigation bar, where it looks like a ribbon with text on it. However, I decided to go with a more minimalistic look, where the navigation bar is a simple rectangle with text on it.

Footer Page

The footer page will be a light grey colour, like the navigation bar, creating visual harmony between the start and end of the page. When users click on the links, it will take them to different wesbites depending on the link they clicked on.

Buttons

The buttons will have a rectangular boarder with rounded edges. The boarder is added to visualize that this is interactable. When users hover over buttons they will slightly increase in size, visualizing the button is interactable.

Reflection

Creating this style guide was a challenging yet rewarding experience. I started by brainstorming the overall look and feel I wanted for my website, focusing on balancing creativity and readability. I explored various design elements, including colour palettes, fonts, and layout options. The process involved refining my ideas to ensure they aligned with my goals of creating an engaging and user-friendly interface.

However, once I began coding the CSS and bringing the design to life, I quickly realised that not everything translated visually the way I had initially imagined. For instance, the navigation bar was originally intended to appear as a ribbon element across the top of the page. While this concept looked appealing in the initial mock-ups, it didn't appear as clean or structured when coded. As a result, I opted for a more minimal and centered navigation layout that better complemented the rest of the site‚Äôs clean and modern composition.

This experience taught me the importance of flexibility when transitioning from design to development. Seeing the site in a real browser environment helped me better understand spacing, alignment, and how different components interact visually. The iterative nature of web design encouraged me to adapt my style guide to suit the practical needs of the website, without losing sight of my original vision.
